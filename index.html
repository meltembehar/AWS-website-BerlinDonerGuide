The story

Berlin's Most Iconic Street Food

The döner kebab as Berliners know it was born in this city. Turkish immigrants in the 1970s reinvented the traditional skewered meat into a handheld sandwich stuffed with vegetables, sauces, and sliced bread — and Berlin has never been the same.

Today, Berlin boasts over 1,600 döner shops — more per capita than any other city in the world. From Kreuzberg's legendary stands to Mitte's modern riffs, every corner hides a new favourite.

Know your döner

Three Classics to Know

Berlin's döner scene isn't one-size-fits-all. Here are the three styles you'll encounter most.

🥙

Ekmek Arası

The OG: meat sliced off a vertical rotisserie and packed into a half-baguette with tomatoes, cucumber, red cabbage, and a choice of sauces. The Berlin classic.

🫓

Dürüm

All the same fillings wrapped tightly inside a thin lavash flatbread and toasted on a griddle. Crunchier, tidier, and popular with the lunch crowd.

🥗

Ismarlama

A customizable plate version served with rice or bulgur, salad and extra sauce. Perfect for those who want more greens and a sit-down experience.

Imren Grill

A Neukölln institution. Family-run since the '80s, their döner plate with house-made chilli sauce has a devoted local following.
